Office LLM-Agent Lab (OfficeClaw)

Mission: We aim to build intelligent office agents that truly understand and automate real-world office workflows—spanning email, calendar, documents, meetings, and cross-application coordination.

Vision: Office work is universal: every industry, every organization, every professional. We believe Office Agent is the most impactful frontier for AI deployment—with both significant academic challenges and real-world product potential.

Unlike general-purpose agent research, we focus exclusively on the office domain. This focus gives us unique competitive advantages: (1) Office-specific benchmarks to define the problem space; (2) Real user feedback to drive continuous improvement; (3) Immediate productization pathways.

Openings: We are looking for highly motivated students to join us. Please email us at yu.yi@officeclaw.ai if interested.

Research Roadmap

Phase 1
Academic Foundation

Focus: Define the Office Agent problem space and establish academic authority.

  • Office Agent Benchmark: Define evaluation standards, release open datasets
  • Office Procedural Memory: Technical foundation for long-horizon office tasks
Output: Benchmark papers (NeurIPS/ICLR), open-source evaluation frameworks
Phase 2
Technical Deepening

Focus: Core technical capabilities and real-user validation.

  • Office Multi-Agent Coordination: Cross-application workflow orchestration
  • Office Agent-User Interaction: Feedback mechanisms, approval workflows
Output: Working prototypes, user study papers, interaction pattern libraries
Phase 3
Closed-Loop Learning

Focus: Self-evolving systems driven by real user data.

  • Office Agent Evolution: Data flywheel, trajectory learning, continuous improvement
  • Office Agent Skills: Productized skill libraries for specific office tools
Output: Beta products, skill marketplace, learning-from-users demonstrations
Phase 4
Product Ecosystem

Focus: Product matrix and industry-specific customization.

  • Email Agent, Calendar Agent, Document Agent, Meeting Agent
  • Industry variants: Healthcare, Legal, Finance, Education
Output: Commercial products, industry partnerships, deployment at scale

Research Directions

All research directions are anchored in Office Agent scenarios, ensuring academic depth with product relevance.

Office Agent Benchmark

Define the Office Agent problem domain: Workspace-Bench, WorkBench, OfficeBench. File dependency reasoning, cross-application workflows, enterprise constraints.

Foundation · Phase 1

Office Procedural Memory

Memory for long-horizon office tasks: project tracking, quarterly reporting, multi-session workflows. State persistence across email/calendar/document contexts.

Memory · Phase 1

Office Multi-Agent Coordination

Cross-application coordination: Email Agent + Calendar Agent + Document Agent. Handoff protocols, shared state, workflow orchestration.

Coordination · Phase 2

Office Agent-User Interaction

Feedback mechanisms for office workflows: approval requests, smart notifications, clarification questions. Human-in-loop design patterns.

Interaction · Phase 2

Office Agent Evolution

Data flywheel for office agents: learning from user corrections, trajectory optimization, continuous improvement. Real-world data drives model refinement.

Evolution · Phase 3

Office Agent Skills

Skill libraries for office tools: Email triage, calendar scheduling, document summarization, meeting transcription. MCP integration with enterprise platforms.

Skills · Phase 3

Why Office Agent?

Academic Opportunity

Office Agent emerged in 2024-2025 with benchmarks like Workspace-Bench, WorkBench, OfficeBench. Top-tier paper opportunities exist in defining problem spaces, proposing solutions, and evaluating systems.

Market Scale

Every professional uses office tools. Email, calendar, documents, meetings—universal across industries. Trillion-dollar market potential for intelligent automation.

Differentiation

Most agent research focuses on general-purpose capabilities. We specialize in Office—giving unique positioning in both academic discourse and product development.

Data Advantage

Office workflows generate rich data: emails, documents, calendar events, meeting transcripts. Real user feedback enables continuous improvement—rare in pure research settings.
